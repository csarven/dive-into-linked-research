Abstract

We call for a community-wide effort to develop more effective means to change and shape the scholarly communication given the state of the Web. We propose a paradigm shift; from information silos to data ownership, from closed and elitist access to knowledge to a sharing and open access culture, from paper user interfaces and locked data to taking advantage of cool human learning capacity, machine processing power, and interaction. Linked Research is the outcome of this framework, and we make our contributions by defining its principles and requirements, cultural change, and building tools for publication and consumption.

We propose an acid test in order to verify, approve, or test the openness, accessibility and flexibility of the approaches for research publication. This is along the lines of a test to distinguish gold from base metals. This test does not mandate a specific technology, therefore the challenge can be met by different solutions. It is intended to test the design philosophies so that different approaches may be closer to passing the independent invention test.

Challenge
  1. Alice makes her research document available on the Web with the research objects available at fine granularity e.g., variables of a hypothesis.
  2. Bob wants to precisely refer to and discuss Alice’s research objects from his own research document.
  3. Carol is interested in discovering research documents in the wild, which contain a pattern of reusable research objects like the ones in Alice, Bob or others’ documents e.g., using the follow your nose type of exploration, searching, or querying against a service.
  4. Dan learns by interacting with the components inside the research document e.g., changing the parameters, rerunning an experiment.
  5. Eve visits Frank, and displays Alice’s article on one of his devices, and prints out a copy.

There are some assumptions (adapted from the Social Web Acid Test - Level 0):

Assumptions
  • The interactions have at least two different tool stacks.
  • The interactions are both human and machine-friendly.
  • All interactions are according to published open standards, with 1) no dependency on proprietary APIs, protocols, or formats, and 2) no commercial dependency or priori relationship between the groups using the workflows and tools involved.
  • All interactions are possible without prior knowledge of the user’s environment or configuration (within reason).
  • There is no spoon.

Call for Linked Research

XXX: This is copied from http://csarven.ca/call-for-linked-research. Perhaps it needs to be simplified. Can we "codify" so that we can verify?

Linked Research [15] is a proposal for Web researchers to use the technologies in the native Web stack to access, share and discover knowledge. The Call for Linked Research [16] aims to encourage the “do it yourself” behaviour for sharing and reusing research knowledge. The workflow template for Linked Research is intended to enable better discovery of research objects, and improved user experience and access to research knowledge.

Linked Research Workflow
  1. URI Ownership: Publish your research and findings at a Web space that you control. This is a fundamental step to control and have responsibility over ones own research. It is inline with the self-archiving act. This serves the purpose of maximizing its accessibility, usage and citation impact.
  2. Web Standards: Publish your progress and work following the Web standards as well as Linked Data design principles. Create a URI for everything that is of some value to you and may be to others e.g., hypothesis, workflow steps, variables, provenance, results. Ensure that the research is cost-free and available via open access. Use a liberal license to promote reuse.
  3. Knowledge Acquisition: Reuse and link to other researchers URIs of value, so nothing goes to waste or reinvented without good reason. It also fosters a navigable network of information, as well as information to flow between research documents.
  4. User Experience: Build towards a strong user experience. Use appropriate media stylesheets. Create a copy of a view for the research community to fulfil organisational requirements. Design interactive user-interfaces for improved communication and education.
  5. Announce: Announce your work publicly so that people and machines can discover it.
  6. Open Feedback: Have an open comment system policy for your document so that any person or machine can give feedback.
  7. Enable: Help, encourage, and motivate others to do the same.

We believe that our Linked Research approach qualifies under enhanced research communication, and invite the community to verify against the proposed framework for article evaluation in The Five Stars of Online Journal Articles [17] or other evaluations as appropriate.

As Linked Data become mainstreamed and widely adopted by the Galleries, Libraries, Museums and Archives (GLAM) communities, currently there are many options for identifiers systems for authors, artists and other types of creators of scholarly, popular and creative worksabc123. Similarly for the scientific and medical research communities, the major researcher identity systems available to date via databases are Scopus Author Identifier (Elsevier) and ResearcherID (Thomson Reuters). However, the data are not open. Nonetheless, both can be linked to the open researcher identifier system, ORCID (Open Researcher and Contributor Identifier). Scholars also tend to deposit their research in different systems and with inconsistency and irregularity of updatesdef456. Linked Research (LR) can remedy this problem as authors will have all their works connected via their unique Web identifiers embedded in their publications or creative works on the Web. Thus they just need to establish one unique Web identifier that can be applied to all of their works, instead of multiple profiles to satisfy many systems as in their current practices. It will also solve the need to disambiguate among authors or creators as their published and creative works on the Web are readily connected to their unique Web identifiers embedded in their works. In short, LR will revolutionize and simplify the way researchers, libraries and archives will manage the researcher identifiers, once adopted and mainstreamed.

Situating Linked Research

This section should break up and move: introduction, problems/ideals, technical considerations..

The Internet and the Web have radically changed the the processes of scholarship Borgman 2007 Scholarship in the Digital Age: Information, Infrastructure, and the Internet . Many forms of scholarship are enabled by these technology changes whether it is the ability to perform social science using social media traces to large scale data processing for physics. However, as previously stated, the formal communication mechanisms used by scholars still do not take full advantage of the capabilities of the web.

Several authors have noted the lack of adoption.

  • Adoption and use of Web 2.0 in scholarly communications. Rob Procter, Robin Williams, James Stewart, Meik Poschen, Helene Snee, Alex Voss, Marzieh Asgari-Targhi Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A 2010 368 4039-4056; DOI: 10.1098/rsta.2010.0155. Published 2 August 2010
  • Calvi, Licia, and Maria Cassella. "Scholarship 2.0: analyzing scholars’ use of Web 2.0 tools in research and teaching activity." Liber Quarterly 23.2 (2013): 110-133.

However, we increasingly see the adoption of web technologies within a parallel communication mechanisms. Samantha Work, Stefanie Haustein, Timothy D. Bowman, Vincent Larivière. Social Media in Scholarly Communication. 2015.

Even with this relatively slow adoption there are signs of web technologies being embraced by the infrastructure that surrounds traditional publication practice. For example, the progressive adoption of the DOI infrastructure for citation of both articles but other artefacts is a strong step towards Linked Research. Both CrossRef and DataCite DOIs support Content Negotiation allowing multiple representations including machine readable representations to be returned. Likewise, ORCID allows for the unique identification of contributors and is available through content negotiations (http://feed.labs.orcid-eu.org).

Indeed, we are beginning to see a linked graph of information around publications based on these DOI focused infrastructures. (See http://blog.crossref.org/2016/01/crossref-the-art-of-cartography.html)

Semantic Web technologies have been widely embraced by the librarian community for organizing traditional scholarly content as well as exposing the rich terminological systems underlying those contents.Library Linked Data in the Cloud: OCLC's Experiments with New Models of Resource Description. Carol Jean Godby, Shenghui Wang, and Jeffrey K. Mixter Synthesis Lectures on the Semantic Web: Theory and Technology 2015 5:2, 1-154 For example, the BIBFRAME initiative aims to ease the transition from MARC metadata records to web enabled metadata systems.

However, these systems are around the periphery of what remains the article. A more important sign of the potential for social movement toward web scholarly native and machine readable scholarly communication ecosystem are the recent recognition of the importance of other research products. For example, the Joint Declaration of Data Citation Principles Data Citation Synthesis Group: Joint Declaration of Data Citation Principles. Martone M. (ed.) San Diego CA: FORCE11; 2014 [https://www.force11.org/datacitation]. explicitly calls for data to be treated the same as papers in the scholarly ecosystem. It also explicitly calls for machine readability. These principle shave endorsed by 100 organizations.

Similarly, the FAIR Data principles for findable, accessible, interoperable and reusable data encourage the semantically interoperable reuse of data.

Beyond data, there are calls for the recognition of software as a critical form of research output. Stan Ahalt, Tom Carsey, Alva Couch, Rick Hooper, Luis Ibanez, Ray Idaszak, Matthew B. Jones, Jennifer Lin, and Erin Robinson. 2015. NSF Workshop on Supporting Scientific Discovery Through Norms and Practices for Software and Data Citation and Attribution. Technical Report. National Science Foundation, , USA.. Approaches for supporting software also leverage the DOI infrastructure (e.g. https://guides.github.com/activities/citable-code/)

Another form of scholarly output that is critical to the Linked Research vision is annotation. The recent announcement of the Annotating All Knowledge Coalition is a major step towards a set of linked annotations that are openly available. The coalition is embracing W3C open annotation standards for interchanging standards.

Other groups are focused on reforming the traditional structure and content of narrative papers into more technically accessible and reusable content. For example, The ReScience Journal makes available the various portions of a paper on Github to be accessed and re-executed if possible. Likewise, the W3C Scholarly HTML working group is focused on the better support of scholarly documents on the web.

Linked Research is additive to these various initiatives by providing a framework for thinking about each how these parts fit together and can be grounded in the web. By establishing a framework, Linked Research can be seen as a both a benchmarking exercise as well as a technique to connect various technologies.

Socially, Linked Research is embedded within the Open Science movement Neylon C, Wu S. Open Science: tools, approaches, and implications. Pac Symp Biocomput. 2009:540-4. However, it explicitly calls out the role of technical choices on meeting open science goals.
